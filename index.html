How to Prevent Fires in Your Home

Although it's frightening to consider losing your house to a fire, there are many things you can take to stop fires from starting. While mishaps may occur, you can learn how to stop a fire from beginning and consuming your house. Let's get going.

Always keep a blanket or fire extinguisher handy

Everyone in the family should be familiar with how to use a fire extinguisher and fire blankets to extinguish small flames before they become larger and more dangerous.

Keep an eye on open flames

We recommend lighting candles throughout the house to create a romantic ambiance, and lighting the fireplace to make the home more cozy. However, it is important to exercise caution and turn out all candles before dozing off, and to keep open flames away from pets.

Make sure your wires are in good shape

Check the wire for damage or chewing before plugging anything into a socket, replace broken wires as soon as possible, and avoid running wires through carpets or other flammable materials.

Check your heating sources

Your home's heating sources may very possibly start a house fire if they aren't operating correctly. Always change the filters in your air conditioner, keep your heaters away from anything that might catch fire, and have a professional inspect these things once a year.
